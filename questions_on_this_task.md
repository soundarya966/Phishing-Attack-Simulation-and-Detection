## Phishing Attack Simulation & Detection

---

## 1. What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of **social engineering attack** where an attacker impersonates a trusted entity to trick users into revealing sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, credit card details, or OTPs.

These attacks are commonly carried out through:
- Emails
- SMS messages
- Phone calls
- Fake websites

The goal of phishing is usually **credential theft, financial fraud, or identity theft**.

---

## 2. What are the Types of Phishing?

Common types of phishing include:

1. **Email Phishing**  
   Mass emails sent to many users pretending to be from legitimate organizations.

2. **Spear Phishing**  
   Targeted phishing attacks aimed at a specific individual or organization.

3. **Whaling**  
   Phishing attacks targeting high-level executives or senior officials.

4. **Smishing**  
   Phishing carried out via SMS or text messages.

5. **Vishing**  
   Voice-based phishing using phone calls.

6. **Clone Phishing**  
   A legitimate email is copied and modified with a malicious link or attachment.

---

## 3. How Can You Detect a Phishing Attack?

Phishing attacks can be detected by identifying common red flags such as:

- Suspicious or mismatched URLs
- Urgent or threatening language
- Generic greetings like “Dear User”
- Poor grammar or spelling mistakes
- Unexpected attachments or links
- Sender email address not matching the official domain

Technical detection methods include:
- Email filtering systems
- URL reputation checks
- Anti-phishing browser extensions

---

## 4. Why is Phishing Dangerous?

Phishing is dangerous because it can lead to:

- Theft of login credentials
- Financial loss
- Identity theft
- Malware or ransomware infections
- Data breaches in organizations
- Unauthorized access to systems

Phishing attacks exploit **human psychology**, making them one of the most effective cyber attack methods.

---

## 5. What Are the Prevention Methods for Phishing?

Phishing can be prevented using a combination of technical controls and user awareness:

### Technical Measures:
- Email spam filters
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- Secure email gateways
- Regular system updates

### User Awareness:
- Verifying sender email addresses
- Avoiding unknown links or attachments
- Checking URLs before clicking
- Cyber security awareness training

---

## 6. What is a Phishing Simulation?

A phishing simulation is a **controlled and ethical exercise** where fake phishing emails are sent in a test environment to evaluate how users respond to phishing attacks.

It helps in:
- Measuring user awareness
- Identifying security gaps
- Training users to recognize phishing attempts

## 9. What Metrics Are Used to Measure a Phishing Campaign?

Common phishing campaign metrics include:
- Emails sent
- Emails delivered
- Emails opened
- Links clicked
- User interaction rate

These metrics help assess how effective a phishing attempt is and where users need more training.

---

## 10. Is Phishing Simulation Ethical?

Yes, phishing simulation is ethical **when performed responsibly**:
- Conducted in controlled environments
- No real credentials are collected
- Users are informed or results are anonymized
- Used only for awareness and training purposes
