# Phishing Attack Simulation & Detection Report

## 1. What is Phishing?
Phishing is a social engineering attack where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, or financial details by impersonating trusted entities via email, messages, or fake websites.

---

## 2. Types of Phishing
- Email Phishing
- Spear Phishing
- Whaling
- Smishing (SMS phishing)
- Vishing (Voice phishing)
- Clone Phishing

---

## 3. Objective of Simulation
The objective of this simulation was to understand how phishing attacks are designed, delivered, and detected in a controlled environment without harming real users.

---

## 4. Phishing Email Template
A fake email was created that mimics a legitimate service and encourages the user to click a link.

**Red flags included:**
- Urgent language
- Suspicious sender email
- Call-to-action link

---

## 5. Landing Page Setup
A fake login page was designed using HTML to simulate a phishing website. The page visually looks legitimate but does not store or transmit credentials.

---

## 6. Campaign Simulation
- Email template was linked to the landing page
- Campaign was launched in a test environment
- User actions such as email opened and link clicked were tracked

---

## 7. Response Tracking
Metrics analyzed:
- Email delivered
- Email opened
- Link clicked

These metrics help understand how users interact with phishing emails.

---

## 8. Identified Phishing Red Flags
- Mismatched URLs
- Poor grammar
- Urgency or threats
- Unexpected attachments
- Generic greetings

---

## 9. Why Phishing is Dangerous
- Credential theft
- Financial fraud
- Identity theft
- Malware infections
- Data breaches

---

## 10. Prevention Methods
- User awareness training
- Email filtering
- Verifying URLs
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- Avoid clicking unknown links

---

## 11. Conclusion
This phishing simulation helped in understanding attacker techniques and emphasized the importance of awareness and preventive measures to defend against phishing attacks.

---

## ⚠️ Ethical Disclaimer
This simulation was conducted strictly for educational purposes and did not involve real victims or real credentials.
